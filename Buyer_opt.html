Model Setup

  • A buyer (B) wants a seller's (S) indivisible good
  • B's valuation is v∼Fvv\sim F_vv∼Fv​, a Myerson regular CDF over [0,1], with mean μ=∫01vdF\mu =\int_0^1 v\mathrm{d}Fμ=∫01​vdF.
  • S's value for good is 0 (i.e. abstract away from production cost)
  • Neither S or B know vvv
  • B chooses a singal structure FFF
  • Observing FFF, S picks a price to maximize his profit
  • Then, B sees signal realization and ppp, decides whether to buy.

Main Results

  • Buyer-optimal signal induces a unit-elastic demand
  • All buyer-optimal outcomes generates efficient trades
  • Buyer-optimal learning is the one that provides the least inforamtion

Profit Maximizing

  • For a signal, FFF. S wants to maximize:
    πF=max⁡p∈[0,1]  p(1−F(p−))\begin{equation*} \pi_F = \max_{p\in [0,1]}\,\, p(1-F(p-)) \end{equation*}πF​=p∈[0,1]max​p(1−F(p−))​
  • The set of profit maximing prices are:
    P(F)=arg⁡max⁡p∈[0,1]  p(1−F(p−))\begin{equation*} P(F) = \arg\max_{p\in [0,1]}\,\, p(1-F(p-)) \end{equation*}P(F)=argp∈[0,1]max​p(1−F(p−))​
  • Consdier the value-probablity space
  • Each poin in (x,y) corresponds to :
    • xxx - a value/price
    • yyy - probability that B's value is less than yyy
  • Hence, each point (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) gives a profit of
    π=x(1−y)\pi = x(1-y)π=x(1−y)
  • Which means the iso-profit curve is y=1−πxy = 1- \dfrac{\pi}{x}y=1−xπ​, for any given profit level π\piπ
  • Giving a terminal condition that values could not exceeds 111, it follows that any iso-profit curve is indeed a truncated Pareto distribution with parameters (π,1)(\pi,1)(π,1)
  • Note that the profit is higher whenver the price is higher and the rejection probability (1-yyy) is lower
  • It follows that profit increases in south-east direction
  • Hence, given a signal FFF (which can be thought as a trading constraint), S wants to find the lowest iso-profit curve, which maxizes his profit
